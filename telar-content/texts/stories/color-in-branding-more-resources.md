---
title: "More resources"
---


-   [Adobe Color,](https://color.adobe.com/create/color-wheel/) by Adobe, you can explore and create color themes/palettes and easily import them into Illustrator; generate a color palette from an image
-   [Colormind.io](http://colormind.io/)
-   [Coolors.co](https://coolors.co/): generate a color palette, adjust the colors, extract colors from images, and other goodies
-   [Paletton](http://paletton.com/): great tool to see color combinations for website design; once you’ve generated a palette, see it in action through the Examples tab; check out the “color blind” option to see how different color combos look to people with various kinds of color blindness
-   [Material Palette](https://www.materialpalette.com/): choose two colors and get an eight-color palette that you can download
-   [Hail Pixel](https://color.hailpixel.com/) by Colordot: a great way to see colors and get hex codes
-   [Multicolr Search Lab](http://labs.tineye.com/multicolr/#colors=95c9be;weights=100;) (by TinEye): choose colors (up to five) and TinEye will return images with those colors
-   [CMYK vs. RGB vs. Pantone](https://medium.com/branding101/colors-pantone-vs-cmyk-vs-rgb-5f0bcdc349cf), by Stephanie Asmus (Medium.com)
-   [10 Color Palettes from the Natural World](https://medium.com/sketch-app-sources/10-color-palettes-from-the-natural-world-to-inspire-your-creative-streak-bc2fb73d161e), by Payman Taei
-   [Pantone Color of the Year](https://www.pantone.com/color-of-the-year/2025): the annual zeitgeist, rendered in color
